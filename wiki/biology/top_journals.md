# Top Journals in Biology

A curated guide to the most prestigious and impactful journals in the biological sciences, organized by scope.

---

## 🏆 Tier 1: Multidisciplinary "Big Three"

These journals publish groundbreaking research across **all** scientific disciplines, including biology. Publishing here is considered a major career milestone.

### Nature
- **Publisher:** Springer Nature (UK)
- **Founded:** 1869
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **Impact Factor:** 48.5 (2024)
- **Editor-in-Chief:** Magdalena Skipper
- **Acceptance Rate:** <8%
- **Access:** Hybrid (subscription + some open access)
- **Notes:** One of the world's most cited and prestigious scientific journals. Covers all natural sciences. Won the Prince of Asturias Award (2007) alongside *Science*. ~3 million unique online readers/month.

### Science
- **Publisher:** American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS, USA)
- **Founded:** 1880
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **Impact Factor:** 45.8 (2024)
- **Editor-in-Chief:** Holden Thorp
- **Acceptance Rate:** <7%
- **Access:** Delayed open access (free after 12 months)
- **Notes:** Founded with support from Thomas Edison and Alexander Graham Bell. Published landmark papers on fruit fly genetics (Morgan), gravitational lensing (Einstein), and the Human Genome Project. ~130,000 subscribers; 400,000+ weekly readers.

### Cell
- **Publisher:** Cell Press / Elsevier
- **Founded:** 1974
- **Frequency:** Biweekly
- **Impact Factor:** 45.5 (2023)
- **Editor-in-Chief:** John Pham
- **Access:** Free after 12 months (from 1995 issues onward)
- **Notes:** Ranked **#1** for highest-impact journal (all fields) over 1995–2005 by ScienceWatch. Covers molecular biology, cell biology, genetics, genomics, immunology, neuroscience, cancer research, and more. More biology-focused than *Nature* and *Science*.

## 🥇 Tier 2: Top Biology-Specific Journals

### PLOS Biology
- **Publisher:** Public Library of Science
- **Founded:** 2003
- **Frequency:** Monthly
- **Impact Factor:** 7.8 (2023)
- **Editor-in-Chief:** Nonia Pariente
- **Access:** **Fully open access** (CC-BY license)
- **Notes:** First journal published by PLOS. Covers all aspects of biology. Promotes article-level metrics over impact factors.

### eLife
- **Publisher:** eLife Sciences Publications Ltd (non-profit)
- **Founded:** 2012
- **Frequency:** Continuous
- **Impact Factor:** 6.4 (2023); journal opposes over-reliance on IF
- **Editor-in-Chief:** Tim Behrens
- **Access:** **Fully open access** (CC-BY)
- **Notes:** Founded by Howard Hughes Medical Institute, Max Planck Society, and Wellcome Trust. Pioneered a "publish, then review" model (2021). Controversially eliminated accept/reject decisions in 2023, instead publishing reviewed preprints with public assessments.

### Current Biology
- **Publisher:** Cell Press / Elsevier
- **Founded:** 1991
- **Frequency:** Biweekly
- **Impact Factor:** ~8.1
- **Notes:** Broad coverage of biology, particularly strong in ecology, evolution, neuroscience, and developmental biology. Known for accessible writing.

### Proceedings of the Royal Society B: Biological Sciences
- **Publisher:** Royal Society (UK)
- **Founded:** 1905
- **Frequency:** Biweekly
- **Impact Factor:** ~4.7
- **Notes:** One of the oldest biology journals in the world. Strong in evolutionary biology, ecology, and behavior.

### Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B
- **Publisher:** Royal Society (UK)
- **Founded:** 1665 (oldest scientific journal in the world)
- **Notes:** Publishes themed issues with invited reviews. Extremely prestigious historically.

### Biological Reviews
- **Publisher:** Cambridge Philosophical Society
- **Impact Factor:** ~11
- **Notes:** Publishes review articles covering all areas of biology. Among the highest-impact review journals in biology.

### BioEssays
- **Publisher:** Wiley
- **Notes:** Publishes review and discussion articles on novel ideas across the molecular life sciences.

## 🔑 Key Takeaways

- **Nature, Science, and Cell** are the "Big Three" — publishing in any of them is a landmark achievement
- **Nature Reviews** series journals have some of the highest impact factors but publish only review articles
- **Open access leaders:** PLOS Biology and eLife are leading the push for free, open science
- **eLife** is experimenting with radical publishing models (no accept/reject, preprint-first)
- **Annual Reviews** series (Annual Review of Biochemistry, etc.) are consistently among the most cited review journals
- The **"Nature family"** (Nature + ~60 specialty journals) and **"Cell family"** (Cell + ~50 specialty journals) dominate modern biology publishing
